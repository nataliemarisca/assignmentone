Web-Accessibility Project: Santa Monica History Museum

DH150 2021 Summer - Project documentation by Natalie Anggawidjaja

About the Project

This project summarizes the research, design process, and reflection of the making of my mock-up Santa Monica History Museum website built throughout DH150 summer 2021 class. It is also to test and improve the web-accessibility of the museum websites.

Research #

First of all, I decided to look up many different museum websites and ran a lighhouse audit report by Chrome Development tool. I decided to choose five museums of a different range of SEO/accessibility scores.

1) Hammer Museum https://hammer.ucla.edu/visit , Accessibility (96), SEO (83)

2) Fowler Museum at UCLA, https://fowler.ucla.edu/ , Accessibility (84), SEO (83)

Santa Monica History Museum, https://santamonicahistory.org/exhibits/ , Accessibility (74), SEO (100)

4) James Bond 007 Museum, http://www.007museum.com/ , Accessibility (56), SEO (80)

5) Ronald Reagan Presidential Library and Museum, https://www.reaganlibrary.gov/ , Accessibility (97), SEO (75)

In general, I have derived on the five museums I have chosen due to their differing range of credibility and SEO/accessibility scores. It has given me a slight insight on what elements affects the outcome of the audit scores and how the presentation and overall design and look of the site correspond to their assigned scores.

Some analysis I found out after running the chrome lighthouse tool is that some bypass block, semantic structure, alt elemetns are really influential on improving the SEO and accessibility score.

Project Design Process #

I have chosen the Santa Monica History Museum for this project and created a mock-up website of it. Throughout the summer, I made a weekly improvement to my website by adding bypass block and semantic structure, color-contrast, tab-index, and ARIA. Below is a more detailed explanation of how I developed and improved on the accessibility and score of my website

semantic structure image

To add some bypass block, I added a 'skip to content' and 'back to top' element. While for the semantic structure, I added several elements such as html with tags for readable texts by including, basic html structures such as !doctype, html, head, style, body, and script, and also basic html content semantics such as nav, header, main, section, article, and footer. I also considered text sizings by implementing h1 to h6 on necessary title texts and added a permalink # to two headings. Lastly, I included a footnote to provide a disclaimer of certain information such as my name, origin website, and SEO/accessibility audit score.

color contrast image

To add some color contrast elements, I included proper css codes for font-size, font-family, link/hypertexts, text-decoration, margin, background-color, text color, and several color variations such as dark-mode, monochromatic mode, and light-mode. I utilised the a color checker called Contrast Ratio to ensure proper compliance with the WCAG 2.1 color contrast ratio of 4:5:1.

color contrast image

To improve on keyboard accessibility, I added about twenty interactive elements including menus, buttons, and bypass blocks. I also added css codes for greater visibility of the bypass block button by including bolder outline. I also added css codes on the styling of focus status on certain interactive items, visual styles on texts for some buttons by including margin, text color, background color, padding, font family, and font size.

color contrast image

To cater to better screen-reader and ARIA, I included a few icon buttons for the purpose of for example direction to social media page, address, font size adjument, and color mode option. I also ensured an easy to understand screen-reader using the Chrome screen reader extension by aria labelling icon buttons, search input box, and any available links. At the start of the page, I included three available features for increasing font-size, decreasing font-size, and color-mode toggle to switch page to dark-mode and back to normal.

In conclusion, I have definitely learnt a tremendous amount of new knowledge from this class in terms of UX design. Being my first Digital Humanities class, this class has provided me with a great introduction to the field. I have been informed of several toolkit to inspect websites such as chrome lighthouse, WAVE evaluation tool, and ARC toolkit. In the original website of my choosing, it has an SEO score of 100 and accessibility score of 74. It also did not have good screen-reader function due to lack of aria-labelling and missing bypass blocks. However, in my mock up website, I have tried my best to improve it and obtained an SEO score of 90 and accessibility score of 98 by adding more adequate codes such as aria label, presence of bypass blocks, choosing a better color contrast, tab-indexing, and semantic structures. Despite not obtaining a perfect 100, I am rather happy with my work and I have started up with zero knowledge and built my way up.
